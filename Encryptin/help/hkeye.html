Key

To encrypt files Encryptin uses the block cipher algorithm AES (Rijndael) used as a standard by the government of the United States of America.

The encryption algorithm needs a key that will be randomly generated by Encryptin.

For this reason, from the KEY OPTIONS
menu you can export or import the key to or from a file. You can also delete the key stored in Google Chrome.

It is very important to save the key. Once the key is lost, it won't be possible to decrypt your encrypted files anymore.

The deletion of a key is irreversible, so is vital to save a copy of the key on your computer or USB stick.


REMEMBER TO BACKUP YOUR KEY.
